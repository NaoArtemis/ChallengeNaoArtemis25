NAO: COACH & CARE


PROJECT GOALS

The NaoArtemis project, created for the NAO Challenge 2025, aims to merge technology with sports to enhance training and accessibility. NAO will monitor athletesâ€™ posture, movements, and vital signs in real time, offering feedback to boost performance and prevent injuries. It will also support mental well-being as a virtual coach with personalized encouragement. The project promotes inclusion, helping fans with disabilitiesâ€”especially individuals with autismâ€”through AAC communication tools. In collaboration with the Audace sports club, these innovations will be tested in real sports environments.

PROPOSED SOLUTIONS

NaoArtemis introduces an AI- and robotics-based system featuring two NAO robots with distinct roles.

Task 1 acts as an assistant coach for athletes. Using computer vision and biometric sensors, it analyzes player movements, generates heatmaps, suggests strategies, and monitors physical conditions to prevent injuries. It also provides motivational support to enhance focus and teamwork.

Task 2 is designed for fans, especially those with disabilities. Positioned in the stands, it offers voice guidance for accessibility and promotes inclusive cheering. It uses AAC and ArUco markers to interpret fan messages, especially from individuals with autism, and responds with related voice outputs. NaoArtemis also aims to raise awareness by using the robots in campaigns that promote inclusion and respect in sports events.
